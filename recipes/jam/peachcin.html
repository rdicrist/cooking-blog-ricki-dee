Ingredients:
  • 3.5 pounds ripe, fresh peaches
  • 2 – 4 cups white sugar
  • 3 – 6 tablespoons pectin
  • ¼ - ½ cup lemon juice (fresh or bottled)
  • Cinnamon to taste
  • 6 half pint mason jars
Instructions:
  1. Remove skins and pits from peaches (using whatever method you want) and cut into roughly ½ inch chunks
  2. Add lemon juice to peaches and put them into large pot on medium-low heat
  3. Cover pot for 3 – 5 minutes
  4. Take cover off and heat an additional 3 – 5 minutes, until peaches are soft enough to mash
  5. Take pot off heat and mash with a potato masher
  6. Repeat steps until you have desired consistency, using a rotary beater if desired
    • NOTE: you will be mashing a final time at the end, so it doesn’t have to be perfect
  7. Mix sugar, cinnamon, and pectin and add to pot, turning heat to medium-low
  8. Stir well and cook, stirring constantly, until the flavors have melded (taste constantly)
    • NOTE: adjust ingredients to taste, start with smallest amount and gradually add until you have the flavor / consistency you want
  9. Bring to medium-high heat and bring to a boil
  10. Boil 2 – 5 minutes, stirring constantly
  11. Take pot off heat and mash a final time
  12. Use ladle to scoop jam into mason jars of desired size, making sure to get an even distribution of fruit chunks
  13. Enjoy!
Notes:
  • Taste often!! All the ingredients are adjustable, except you have to keep at least a one-half-to-one sugar-to-fruit ratio
  • Jam will get firmer after it cools
  • Eat within ~4 months~ of cooking, keep refrigerated
